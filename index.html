VMI – Visual Mind Illusion

A Hidden Human Ability Described by MD Joy – Founder of Sounth

💠 What Is VMI?

VMI (Visual Mind Illusion) is a hidden mental ability that causes imagination to leave the mind and briefly appear in the real world — seen through the person’s own eyes. It’s not a hallucination or a dream, but a visual illusion created by intense emotional energy and mental focus.

🧠 How It Happens

VMI can activate when a person is feeling nervous, excited, or emotionally unstable. In this state, the left side of the brain becomes overloaded (like low RAM). The person imagines something deeply — and suddenly, they see it outside of themselves, as if it’s real, for 2–4 seconds. Then comes a deep fear: “What is happening with me?”

🔄 From Accidental to Habit

If the person keeps imagining after this first event, the brain starts to create a small mental path — a root — for this ability. After 2–3 activations, the root becomes stronger. Now the person can trigger VMI without strong emotion. Faces, shapes, objects, or even full visual thoughts may appear in the real world as illusions.

🧩 Left Brain & Human Type

VMI is most active in people with dominant left-brain function. It’s common among those who had strong imaginations in childhood, and less common in intelligent or emotionally balanced humans. Most people who experience VMI don’t understand what it is — they ignore it or forget it happened.

🧨 The Danger of Misuse

People who develop strong control over VMI may use it to deceive, manipulate, or disturb others. Because it’s invisible to others, there’s no clear proof when it’s used. This makes it dangerous in the hands of the wrong people — especially those with low emotional control or negative intentions.

📢 Why the World Needs to Know

VMI is real. It’s invisible, misunderstood, and often ignored — but it affects how the mind works and how people interact.
